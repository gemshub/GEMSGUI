Activity (relative)
 
Defined by the equation a = exp[( m  -   mo)/RT] where R is the gas constant, T the thermodynamic temperature, m the chemical potential and mo the standard chemical potential, the definition of which depends on the choice of the standard state.
 
Source: IUPAC Compendium of Chemical Terminology 2nd Edition (1997) 
 

Activity Coefficient  f, g
 
The activity coefficient fB of a substance B in a liquid or solid mixture containing mole fractions xB, xC, ... of the substances B, C, ... is a dimensionless quantity defined in terms of the chemical potential mB of B in the mixture by: 
RT ln (xBfB) = mB (cd, T, P, x) - mB * (cd, T, p) 
where x denotes the set of mole fractions xB, xC, ... . The activity coefficient gB of a solute B in a solution (especially a dilute
liquid solution) containing molalities mB, mC, ... of solutes B, C, ... in a solvent A is a dimensionless quantity defined in terms of the chemical potential mB by:
RT ln (mB gB/m ) = mB – { mB - RT ln (mB/m )}inf     (inf means infinity) 
Source: IUPAC Compendium of Chemical Terminology 2nd Edition (1997)

Phase, symmetric
In symmetric phases (i.e., gas mixture), concentrations, activity coefficients and standard chemical potentials are expressed in one and the same (usually, mole fraction) scale.

Phase, asymmetric
In asymmetric phases (i.e., aqueous electrolyte, sorption phase) only components of a solvent (carrier) are treated in mole fraction scale, whereas solutes (sorbates) are normalized to certain quantity of this carrier (e.g. molality scale).
