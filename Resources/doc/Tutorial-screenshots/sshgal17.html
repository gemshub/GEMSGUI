(28) Working with chemical thermodynamic database of your modelling project.

Sooner or later, you may find that out your modelling project database has to be extended with another chemical element, some chemical species of it, and some solid phases of those. In GEM-Selektor, such extensions are easy to do using its , "Thermodynamic Database" mode of operation. To get there,  you should close the modelling project by clicking the "Back" button on the GEM Modelling Project dialog.


This will get you back to GEMS Main dialog (below) that also appears when you start GEM-Selektor again. 

Note, however, than in the latter case, in order to get access to the "Carbonate" modelling project database,
you will first have to open the project and immediately close it when the above dialog will appear.


 
Now, on the GEMS Main Dialog (above), click the "Thermodynamic Database" button.
You will get a Database Management dialog shown below:


From this dialog, you can get access (via screen forms) to six record chains that comprise a thermodynamic part of the GEM-Selektor database. By using their module screenforms, database records can be edited, created, re-configured, checked (calculated), "cloned", deleted, copied, moved and renamed.

Multiple records can also be backed up into text format files, restored from backup files, as well as exported or imported using format scripts.
