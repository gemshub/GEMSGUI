(13) After the equilibrium state has been calculated and saved in the database, you will see a "Results" page (tab) of the "Single Thermodynamic System" dialog (if you see another window, click at the "SysEq" icon on the left-hand side):



This page shows a list of all potentially present phases (aq_gen denotes aqueous electrolyte, gas_gen - gas mixture); number of species (components) included in each phase; and the mole amount of each phase at equilibrium.

Stable phases appear in positive mole quantities, with stability indexes (logSI) close to zero; unstable phases - in zero quantities, with significantly negative logSI values. Note that, even though there were three initial metal carbonate ingredients (CaCO3, FeCO3 and MnCO3), only one (Calcite) appears as a stable solid phase at equilibrium. Iron appears as Goethite phase (FeOOH), and manganese - as Pyrolusite (MnO2) instead of the respective carbonates Siderite FeCO3 and Rhodochrosite MnCO3 whose compositions were initially given as recipe ingredients.

The reason, of course, is the oxidizing gas atmosphere - the gas phase containing 20% O2 is present in a large excess (almost 10400 moles). This results in pe = 12.29, visible in the Status line in the lower part of the dialog. Both iron and manganese carbonate phases would be stable only in the absence of gaseous oxygen, at reducing conditions.   

(14) Each phase entry can be expanded to see dependent components (species) in this phase (below). To expand/shrink the phase, mouse-click on the "+"/"-" sign on the list view tree. 



Now, in the "Amount (mol)" column, you can see mole amounts x of species (components) present at equilibrium in each phase. The amount of a phase is actually the sum of amounts of all its components. To the right, the activity values (calculated using the GEM dual solution vector u) for phase components are shown. For gases, these numbers are actually fugacities, and for single-component solids they mean the plain saturation index (1 - saturated, < 1 - under-saturated, > 1 - super-saturated). The last column shows the calculated activity coefficients (1 in any phase with simple ideal mixing).

On the above screenshot, you can see that the system is saturated with respect to calcite, and 19.3 orders of magnitude under-saturated to Siderite (Sd). The system is also slightly under-saturated to aragonite.

In the "Concentration" column, the mole fractions of gases in the gas phase are pretty much the same as those usually present in the atmosphere.

By expanding the "aq_gen" phase, you can also examine the equilibrium aqueous speciation (not shown); note that activity and concentration for aqueous species are expressed in practical (molal, mol/kgH2O) scale.

To see other results of our model calculation, click on the "Open EqDemo window..." toolbar button or "View" "GEM IPM task results..." menu item (proceed to the next page of this screenshot tutorial).
