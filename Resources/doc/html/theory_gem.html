1. The convex programming approach to chemical equilibria

GEM-Selektor is an implementation of a convex programming approach to Gibbs energy minimization calculations of chemical equilibria ([1981KAR]; [1997KAR/CHU]; [2000KUL]; [2001KAR/CHU]; [2002KUL]). In brief, this approach is an advanced numerical technique for thermodynamic modeling of heterogeneous aquatic (geo)chemical systems, especially those involving metastability and dispersity of mineral phases, solid solution - aqueous solution equilbria, and adsorption/ion exchange. More details can also be found in the TM-44-02-06 PSI Technical report (available as pdf files in the /program/doc/pdf/  or from the GEMS-PSI web page).


2. Karpov (Kuhn-Tucker) conditions, primal and dual GEM solutions   

The Convex Programming approach uses the Kuhn-Tucker conditions and the Duality Theorem (details in Karpov, Chudnenko and Kulik 1997, Amer.J.Sci. v.297, p.767-806) to detect when a global minimum of the total Gibbs energy function G(x) is achieved. This yields two kinds of results simulataneously: the primal solution vector x (speciation, mole amounts of chemical species in all stable phases) and the dual solution vector u (chemical potentials of stoichiometry units). A detailed description can also be found in TM-44-02-06 PSI Technical report (available as Appendix in the pdf file in /program/doc/pdf/  or from the GEMS-PSI web page).


3. The Interior Points Method (IPM) algorithm

GEMS numerical kernel is an Interior Points Method (IPM) module for non-linear minimization of total Gibbs energy of the heterogeneous multi-phase chemical system. The IPM uses internally the Kuhn-Tucker conditions of G(x) minimum.  Since GEMS-PSI version 1.94a, this kernel comes optimized for the mass balance precision, convergence, and extended input data diagnostics as an  enhanced GEM IPM-2 module  to meet the needs of the most demanding modelling applications related to performance assessment in nuclear waste disposal or in aquatic geochemistry. Theoretical description with some examples can be found in the TM-44-02-06 PSI Technical report (available as  pdf files in /program/doc/pdf/  directory or from the GEMS-PSI web page).

 

4. Calculation of activity coefficients in non-ideal mixed phases 

On each iteration of the IPM algorithm, activity coefficients of chemical species in non-ideal multi-component phases must be calculated. For some common activity models, several built-in functions are available in GEM-selector code (see the  Activity-Coeffs.pdf  manual.). If the required model is not available as a built-in fucntion, the user can always create his/her own equations for activity coefficients as math scripts in Phase definition records.


5. Simulation of irreversible mass-transfer processes 

GEM-Selektor module "Process" can generate and plot large sequences of chemical equilibrium states to simulate the irreversible chemical mass-transfer using principles of local and partial equilibria.  Kinetic dependencies can also be included into Process generation math scripts because the GEM IPM algorithm accepts upper-and lower restrictions to mole amount of any chemical species in the system.

6. Dual-Thermodynamic calculations (under construction)

The Karpov (Kuhn-Tucker) necessary and sufficient conditions of chemical equilibrium lead to the dual solution of GEM  problem and thus - to the so-called "Dual Thermodynamic" calculations that can be performed when some of computed elements of the GEM primal solution (vector x) or their functions (concentration, activity coefficients, activities) are independently known from experiments or geochemical data. For instance, from the DualT calculation, one can retrieve either the unknown molar Gibbs energy G* values for pure  solid-solution end-members or their activity coefficients by modelling only equilibria in the co-existing aqueous electrolyte phase. If several experiments at different phase compositions are available, then DualT calculations also permit to find the "best" end-member stoichiometry and retrieve uncertainty intervals of G* or activity coefficient estimates.

To facilitate such calculations and help the users to do it efficiently, the DualTh module of GEM-Selektor is now under construction. It will be available in GEM-Selektor v.3-PSI.


7. Uncertainty Space calculations (under construction)

In geochemical modelling, all the input data are usually treated as deterministic, though in reality, neither the standard molar Gibbs energy of components nor bulk chemical composition of the system are known precisely. Without a sensitivity study, geochemical modelling may potentially lead to serious misinterpretations or totally wrong conclusions. This motivated our on-going work on a new approach to geochemical modelling under uncertainty of input data initiated by a paper [Karpov I.K. et al., Russ. Geol. Geophys. 1999, v.40, p. 971-978].

Briefly, (more in Theoruns.html <under construction>) the new "uncetrtainty" approach combines (i) Gibbs energy minimization (GEM) algorithm for calcula-tion of heterogeneous chemical equilibria [Karpov et al., 1997; 2001]; (ii) sampling the multi-dimensional “uncertainty space” using a uniform probing grid; (iii) advanced statistical analysis based on the “decision-making” criteria adopted from the game theory. “Sampling” means here GEM calculations of scores to thousands equilibrium states at different combinations of uncertain input data, located within an uncertainty space made of the respective uncertainty intervals taken as coordinates. An advanced analysis (using statistical criteria similar to that from games theory and decision-making support mathematics) is needed because of a vast sampled output (full chemical speciation at each point) and, mainly, because the a priori correct phase assemblage (or experimental concentration) may not be the most frequent one(s) among sampled results.  

The pilot studies performed so far [Chudnenko K.V. et al., PSI Bericht in preparation] shows that the new simulation technique indeed reveals well-structured  "uncertainty space" with features that are persistent to the downscaling or the enhanced-resolution sampling. The graphical analysis of those features appears to yield trustful results that lead to a significant reduction of the initial input uncertainty intervals, especially if the available experimental data has been used for filtering the sampled points. This, in turn, makes the GEM modelling results much more robust and their interpretation - more reliable.

To facilitate GEM modelling under uncertainty and enhance modelling productivity, the UnSpace module of GEM-Selektor is under construction. It is supposed to be available in GEM-Selektor v.3-PSI.
