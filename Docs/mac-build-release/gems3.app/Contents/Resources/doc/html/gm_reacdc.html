1. Reaction-Defined Data Format for Dependent Components

ReacDC records can be used for defining thermodynamic properties of aqueous or surface complexes and minerals, for which thermochemical data are not available or predicted data are too uncertain. For such species, only logK of the dissolution, association or surface complexation reaction is usually known at 25 oC or other temperatures.

A ReacDC record describes a reaction between dependent components (species) with stoichiometry coefficients and standard molar properties (dGr, dHr, dSr, dCpr, dVr), which set up a certain T,P  dependence of logK. Because the GEM IPM algorithm cannot use reactions and their equilibrium constants to calculate the equilibrium speciation in phases, each ReacDC record also provides a set of standard molar (partial molal) thermodynamic properties for a "new" species. These standard properties of the "new" species are computed from standard molar properties of the reaction and those of all other species involved in the reaction. The "new", reaction-defined species, actually, gives a name to the reaction in the form of ReacDC record key. Thermodynamic properties of the new species will be automatically maintained consistent to those of other species involved in the reaction at all feasible T,P values. There are several methods of calculation of thermodynamic properties of reaction-defined species at elevated T,P.

When a ReacDC record for a "new" species is created, the "involved" species must already exist in the project database as either DComp or ReacDC records. Any reaction defined in a ReacDC record can refer to other ReacDC records, thus forming a "reaction chain"; up to seven levels of such recursion are supported, i.e. the ReacDC record for an "involved" species can refer to another "involved" ReacDC, and so on, up to 7 levels. Any reaction chain always terminates with a reaction that refers to DComp records only.

  The ReacDC module and data format is a unique feature of GEM-Selektor, to our knowledge not yet available in other codes for equilibria calculations or for maintaining thermodynamic databases. Accordingly, GEM-Selektor database is the first "hybrid" thermodynamic database in a sense that thermochemical data and reactions are used together for any kind of geochemical modeling while the consistency is maintained.

Therefore, new thermodynamic datasets (e.g., for surface complexation or aqueous complexes of trace metals) can be developed using ReacDC and reaction logK values at 1 bar 25 oC using the so-called "isoelectric/isocoulombic reactions approach", believed to provide good extrapolation of logK up to 250-300 oC (Gu et al., 1994; Kulik,2002,a,b). For instance, the cation exchange reaction CaCO30 + Cd+2 = Ca+2 + CdCO30 can be used for determining standard partial molal properties of CdCO30 aqueous complex from that of Cd+2 and Ca+2 ions and CaCO30 complex, available from the Slop98.dat dataset (Shock et al., 1997) (imported into the default GEM-Selektor database). The above reaction is isocoulombic because equal number of equal charges compensate on both sides. This is a one-term temperature extrapolation because only logK298 is specified, thus dGr = dHr = const, dSr = 0 and dCpr = 0 at all temperatures. This reaction provides a much better temperature extrapolation than a corresponding association reaction (Cd+2 + CO3-2 = CdCO30) because it actually correlates properties of CdCO30 complex with those of a similar species CaCO30, while the effects such as activity coefficients or solvation energies largely cancel out.


1.1. ReacDC module calculations

After the wizard is finished, you should see Page 1 of the ReacDC module window. The third record key field (species name) appears in the RDname field; we recommend you to enhance this comment line. The second line (RDform) is still empty, but it is obligatory to enter there the chemical formula of the "new" species. When ready, fill out a vector  SC_DC of the stoichiometry coefficients of all species involved in the reaction (the species names in the form of DComp/ReacDC record keys are visible in the REsDC list to the right; the "new" ReacDC-defined species is always the last one in the list). The convention is that stoichiometry coefficients of reactants are negative, and those of products are positive real numbers. For instance, in a reaction Cd+2 + H2O = Cd(OH)2,aq + 2H+, stoichiometry coefficients are -1, -1, +1 and +2, respectively.

After the reaction has been formulated, you must set up its standard properties in V0r[0]; G0r[0] or logKr[0] or logKr[1]; S0r[0] or H0r[0]; and Cp0r[0] cells. Zeros are valid quantities here, and any cell with unknown data must be set as an EMPTY value ("---" or "`"). Note that, out of three possibilities of entering the Gibbs energy effect of the reaction: as dG0 (G0r[0] cell), logK (logKr[1] cell), and K (equilibrium constant in logKr[0] cell), you can use only one, and two other must be filled with EMPTY values (they will be automatically calculated later). Likewise, you can enter either S0r[0] or H0r[0] but another must be set to EMPTY.

After you have entered the standard effects of the reaction (and EMPTY values where necessary), click on the "Calculate..." toolbar button or press F9 or execute the "Record" "Calculate" menu command. The program may ask you to save the ReacDC record first (always a good idea if the record is just created). Which exactly calculations are to be performed and what input data will be used, is defined by the method codes which can be changed in Remake mode only.

In a moment, you will see a complete set of standard properties  for the reaction and a "new" species on Page 1. If some input data were inconsistent or insufficient, you will get warnings or error messages. If this is the case, stay on the safe side and reject insertion of calculated values, then check the input values and try calculation again. After successful calculation, you can see on Page 2 the coefficients of logK = f(T) equation (in the aiLgKr vector), computed from standard properties of the reaction. In the TCint_ and P_int fields to the left, enter the ranges of applicability of the obtained temperature/ pressure extrapolation (usually from 0 to 1000 bar and from 0 to 200 or 300 oC). When finished, save the record to database. Now, you can browse other records or "clone" to create a new ReacDC record. 

In most cases (by default), ReacDC records are set to define the so-called 3-term temperature extrapolation with dCpr(T) = const where non-zero S0r[0] and Cp0r[0] must be known in addition to the Gibbs energy effect (or logK) of the reaction at 1 bar 25 oC. If  the heat-capacity effect dCpr = 0 then one has the 2-term (Van't Hoff ) extrapolation, and if also the entropy effect dSr =0, the 2-term extrapolation reduces to a 1-term one with dGr(T) =dHr(T) = const. Alternatively, if dCpr = 0 and dHr = 0, one obtains a 1-term extrapolation with logK(T) = const and non-zero dS0.

2. ReacDC Create/Remake Wizard

To construct a reaction in ReacDC record, close your modelling project and switch to "Thermodynamic Database" mode of GEM-Selektor. There, open the "Dependent Components (ReacDC)" module window and execute the "Record" "Create" menu command. In the appearing "ReacDC: Insert a new record key" dialog, make a new ReacDC record key (at best selecting one of existing keys "From List" and modifying it), and press "Ok". This record key also denotes a "new" dependent component to be defined in this ReacDC record. 

Now, the ReacDC wizard appears (see below sections 2.1 to 2.4). There you can configure this ReacDC instance and select other substances involved in the reaction.

After the wizard is finished, you should see Page 1 of the ReacDC module window. Please, follow the instructions in Section 1.1.

2.3. ReacDC Wizard Step 3 - Specific dimensions and settings

Normally, there is no need to change something in this wizard page. However, in two cases, the changes are needed.

(1) ReacDC calculation can be performed to retrieve the properties of reaction made of DComp or ReacDC defined substances already present in the accessible (project) database. To achieve this (after selecting those substances in the previous wizard page), locate the counter "Number of DCs involved in the reaction" and reduce this number by one. This needs to be done because by default, this number contains also the 'n' (new) substance to be defined in this ReacDC record by entering some known standard properties of the reaction. However, if all substances involved in the reaction already have DComp or ReacDC records then all standard effects (properties) of the reaction can be immediately calculated.

(2) It is possible (as done in some thermodynamic data bases) to set the temperature and pressure dependencies of the logK of reaction using the interpolation grid. If this is the case (method codes are set to KZZ) then set the required number of T and P grid points in the counters appearing in the group "Dimensions toset only for the logK array for T,P...". The values can be entered later in the ReacDC window, Page 2. The built-in spline interpolation function will be used in internal RTParm and SysEq calculations.

4. Data Entries on ReacDC Window Pages


RDname - Full name of reaction and reaction-defined DC (species)
   
This is a string up to 70 characters long, entered by the user. It serves only for identification and commenting purposes on the ReacDC window page. Examples: 
>OZn+  inner-sphere surface complex of Zn+2 on HFO  
  
 
RDform - Chemical formula of reaction-defined DC (species), input
   
This is one of the most important input data field on the ReacDC screen forms. Enter here a chemical formula of the species, expressed in symbols of Independent Components (IComp) at present available in the database. The formula completely defines stoichiometry and charge of the reaction-defined Dependent Component. Formulae are parsed and analyzed in many modules of GEM-Selektor to be converted into lines of the stoichiometry matrix, for calculation of molar masses, phase/species compatibility checks etc. GEM-Selektor supports a simple and convenient formula syntax, very close to the conventional chemical notation.
 
Formula is a string of text, maximum 70 characters long. Only symbols of Independent Components (IC) from currently available IComp records can be used. Each IC symbol begins with an upper-case Latin letter, followed by zero to four lower-case Latin letters or _ (underscore) characters. Explicit valence of IC can be specified within vertical bars || immediately after the IC symbol, e.g. H2S|-2| (hydrogen sulfide).
  
IC symbols with stoichiometry coefficients can be grouped in brackets ([]) followed by a common stoichiometry coefficient, e.g. Th(OH)4. Up to 3 levels of brackets can be nested into each other. On translation of the formula, the stoichiometry coefficient after brackets will be applied to each of IC symbols inside. For instance, writing Eu2(CO3)3 is equivalent to Eu2C3O9. Stoichiometry coefficients (except charge) must be positive but not necessarily integer (decimal point notation is allowed, e.g. 2.67). Absence of a formula stoichiometry coefficient after the IC symbol means that this coefficient equals 1.

The atomic mass of an isotope must be specified in slashes // immediately before an IC symbol, e.g. /12/C. Charge (+, +2, ..., -, -2, ...,@) must always be provided in formulae of aqueous species and surface complexes.  Charge can also be non-integer, and it will be always addressed to the Zz  independent component.
 
More examples of species chemical formulae:
Na+, Cl-, NaCl@, 00.5Zn+        aqueous or surface species
SiO2, Fe3Si4O10(OH)2               quarts, minnesotaite
Fe|3|2O3                                    hematite
/3/H2/18/O                                    isotopic form of water
 The RDform field is processed when you click on "Calculate" icon or "Record" "Calculate" menu item in the ReacDC window. You may get error messages if something is wrong with the formula stoichiometry or charge. SC_DC - Stoichiometry coefficients of species (DC) in the reaction (reactants < 0, products > 0)
     
This input vector contains user-entered stoichiometry coefficients of chemical species involved in the reaction.
The coefficients are real numbers that correspond to the following convention: 
 s0(DC0) + s1(DC1) + ... + sM-1(DCM-1) +  sM(DCM) = 0, 
where s0, s1, ... are stoichiometry coefficients, and M <= 16. The new reaction-defined species must always be the last one (DCM). By convention used in GEM-Selektor, stoichiometry coefficients of reactants are negative and those of  products - positive.  The "new" reaction-defined species can be either a product or a reactant, depending the sign of its stoichiometry coefficient.
 
This version of GEMS does not check the validity of s0, s1, ... coefficients relative to chemical formulae of all species involved (even though it is mathematically possible). Hence, the user is fully responsible to provide correct stoichiometry coefficients and assign correct signs to them.

4.1. Coefficients of logK = f(T) equations

 aiLgKr - Coefficients of the logK = f(T) equation

This dynamic vector will be allocated on create/remake when the "K" code is entered in REcMod[0] cell, the modification of method is set in REcMod[1] cell, and the flag RE_arr[0] is turned on.
For a reaction describing an ordinary (non-isotopic) component (codes "1", "2", "3" or "L" in the REcMod[1] cell), the following form of logK=f(T) equation is applied:
logK = a0 + a1*T + a2/T + a3*lnT + a4/T^2 + a5*T^2 + a6/T^0.5
where T is absolute temperature (K), and  a0, ..., a6 are the empirical coefficients functionally related to dGr, dHr, dSr, dCpr  of the reaction (see Nordstrom & Munoz 1994). Default value of all coefficients is 0.
For a pseudo-reaction defining an isotopic form of compound, ("G" code set in the REcMod[1] cell ), another form of lnK" = f(T) equation will be applied:
1000*lnK" = a0 + a1/T + a2/T^2 + a3/T^3 + a4/T^4
where K" stands for a fractionation constant of an isotope between this compound and the element in its standard state, T is taken in Kelvin, and  a0, ..., a4 are the empirical coefficients (default value is 0).
aiLgKr[0] -  a0 coefficient of logK = f(T) equation (or 1000*lnK" = f(T) for an isotopic compound)
    
For non-isotopic species, a non-zero value of this coefficient will be automatically calculated from 3-term extrapolation and from 1-term extrapolation at dHr = 0. In the latter case, other coefficients will be zeros (logK = const).
 
aiLgKr[1] - a1 coefficient in the a1*T term of logK = f(T) equation (for isotopic species: in the a1/T term)
For non-isotopic species, a non-zero value of this coefficient requires that dCpr of reaction changes with temperature.

aiLgKr[2] - a2 coefficient in the a2/T term of logK = f(T) equation (for isotopic species: in the a2/T^2 term)
For non-isotopic species, a non-zero value of this coefficient will be automatically calculated from 2 or 3-term extrapolations, as well as from 1-term extrapolation at dSr = 0. In the latter case, other coefficients will be zeros (dGr = const).
   
aiLgKr[3] - a3 coefficient in the a3*lnT term of logK = f(T) equation (for isotopic species: in the a3/T^3 term)
  For non-isotopic species, a non-zero value of this coefficient will be automatically calculated from 3-term extrapolations. 
  
aiLgKr[4] -  a4 coefficient in the a4/T^2 term of logK = f(T) equation (for isotopic species: in the a4/T^4 term)

aiLgKr[5] - a5 coefficient in the a5*T^2 term of logK = f(T) equation
 
aiLgKr[6] - a6 coefficient in the a6/T^0.5 term of logK = f(T) equation

Each column of this data object has 5 cells for input coefficients of temperature power terms in the isobaric heat capacity dCpr  = f (T  )  equation of the following form:

E00RErem: Illegal link (q>=8)!
 
Memory corruption, or recursion of ReacDC records exceed the limit (7).  
  
Action: Reconsider links inside ReacDC records. If recursion is less than 7 then restart the GEMS code and try again. If this does not help, please, send a bug report.

               Press "Proceed" to continue (possible for a "fictive" new species only);

                       only one cation and up to 4 ligands are allowed.

This usually happens when a new project has been created by copying records from the default database or another project (copying automatically changes the last field of record keys).
